    The Ember I Carried

The house I was born into was sick long before I arrived.
Anger lived in the walls like mold.
Every room seemed to breathe hostility.
Doors slammed harder than hearts opened.
Love was unpredictable there —
sometimes a blanket,
sometimes a blade.


I grew up learning that peace was temporary
and survival was a skill.

So I spent years trying to escape.


I ran toward distance,
toward new faces, new places,
new versions of myself.
I convinced myself that if I got far enough away,
the past would lose my scent.
Every step felt like a victory.
Every mile felt like another link
broken from the chain.


But some things do not follow you.

They travel as you.


One day, life became still enough for me to notice.
No dramatic revelation. No shattered mirror.
Just a glimpse.


I saw it in the way I pushed people away
when they got too close.
In the way my anger arrived
before my vulnerability could.
In the way I treated love like something
that needed to be gripped tightly
before it disappeared.

And suddenly, I recognized something familiar.


The same storm.
The same fire.
The same shadow I thought I had left behind.


The monster I had been running from
was never waiting at the end of the road.
A piece of it had been walking beside me
the entire time.

Not as a face.
Not as a memory.
As reflex.
As instinct.
As wounds that had learned my name.


That was the cruelest discovery of all.

I thought I was carrying scars from the fire.
I didn't realize I was carrying embers.


And everywhere I went,
they came with me,
waiting for enough wind
to glow again.


    -Aun Zafar
