    A Beautiful Ache

She moves through my thoughts
like moonlight drowning in river water,
beautiful because it cannot be held.


I find her everywhere.
In the trembling hush before rain.
In the last gold breath of sunset
caught on empty streets.
In songs that sound like old wounds
learning how to sing again.


I loved her quietly.
The way forests love the fog,
without asking it to stay.
The way the sea reaches for the shore
only to be left behind
again and again.


Her laughter
felt like spring arriving
to a house abandoned by winter.
Her eyes carried whole constellations,
and I was foolish enough
to mistake their light for home.


But some stars are born
only to be admired from earth.
No prayer can lower them.
No longing can teach the sky
how to surrender.


So I keep her
like pressed flowers inside a forgotten book,
fragile and untouched by time.
A beautiful ache.
A language my heart still speaks
even in silence.


And if love is a kind of ruin,
then let me be the cathedral
with her name still echoing
through the broken walls.


    -Aun Zafar
